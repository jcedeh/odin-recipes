Loaded Biscuit Bomb Muffins

Description

These loaded biscuit bomb muffins are filled with crisp bacon, Colby Jack cheese, and green onions, baked in muffin tins until fluffy and golden with a sprinkling of everything bagel seasoning.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 pound bacon
  • 2 (16.3 ounce) cans refrigerated biscuit dough
  • 2 cups shredded Colby Jack cheese
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons everything bagel seasoning

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Line 16 wells of standard muffin tins with cupcake liners.
  2. Place bacon in a large skillet and cook over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, until evenly browned, about 10 minutes. Drain bacon slices on paper towels; crumble when cool enough to handle.
  3. Carefully stretch each biscuit into a circle about 4 inches in diameter. Fill each one with 1 tablespoon bacon, 2 tablespoons cheese and 1 to 2 teaspoons green onions. Wrap edges of dough around fillings and pinch together to seal, forming a ball. Place each biscuit seam side down into prepared muffin tins.
  4. Brush the top of each biscuit with egg and sprinkle evenly with bagel seasoning.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until golden, 15 to 17 minutes.
